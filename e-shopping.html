why we are different?

To stay afloat in the face of the Covid pandemic threat, many small firms have been compelled to re-evaluate their decades-old established business practises. Future-focused firms, on the other hand, are taking a closer look at the challenges that come from these new digital business skillsets and how to handle them. A new normal of lockdowns led to more firms and customers going “digital,” boosting the percentage of e-commerce in global retail trade from 14 percent in 2019 to 17 percent in 2020. Since last year, restaurants, movie theatres, and gyms have mainly reopened after a series of lockdowns that were common. While children are still out of school, many in-person companies are experiencing personnel shortages (whether it be a school requirement or a desire of parents). The virus is still a consideration in many office employees’ decision-making, as seen by the hundreds of LinkedIn postings about returning to work while the infection is still a concern. Contrary to popular belief, the situation is still evolving fast, despite the term “post-Covid.” Customers’ buying habits are changing, but retailers and e-commerce companies are seeing that the developments of the last year are here to stay.
